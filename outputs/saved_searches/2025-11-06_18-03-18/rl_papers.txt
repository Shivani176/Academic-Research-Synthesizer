I found several recent papers on transformer attention mechanisms from both ArXiv and OpenAlex. Here are the key findings:

## Recent ArXiv Papers (2021-2025):

### 1. **Generalized Probabilistic Attention Mechanism in Transformers** (2024)
- **Authors**: DongNyeong Heo, Heeyoul Choi
- **Key Innovation**: Introduces GPAM (Generalized Probabilistic Attention Mechanism) that allows negative attention scores while preserving fixed total sum
- **Problem Addressed**: Tackles rank-collapse and gradient vanishing issues in conventional attention
- **Applications**: Language modeling and neural machine translation

### 2. **Continuous-Time Attention: PDE-Guided Mechanisms for Long-Sequence Transformers** (2025)
- **Authors**: Yukun Zhang, Xueqing Zhou
- **Key Innovation**: Infuses partial differential equations (PDEs) into attention mechanism
- **Benefits**: Handles extremely long sequences with polynomial rather than exponential decay
- **Mechanism**: Attention weights evolve over pseudo-time via diffusion, wave, or reaction-diffusion dynamics

### 3. **From Cognition to Computation: A Comparative Review of Human Attention and Transformer Architectures** (2024)
- **Authors**: Minglu Zhao, Dehong Xu, Tao Gao
- **Focus**: Comparative analysis between human attention and Transformer attention
- **Insights**: Explores differences in capacity constraints, attention pathways, and intentional mechanisms

### 4. **Adaptive Sparse and Monotonic Attention for Transformer-based ASR** (2022)
- **Authors**: Chendong Zhao et al.
- **Application**: Automatic Speech Recognition
- **Innovation**: Integrates sparse attention and monotonic attention for streaming ASR
- **Benefits**: Improves attention mechanism for speech recognition benchmarks

## Recent OpenAlex Papers:

### 5. **Informer: Beyond Efficient Transformer for Long Sequence Time-Series Forecasting** (2021)
- **Authors**: Haoyi Zhou et al.
- **Key Features**: 
  - ProbSparse self-attention with O(L log L) complexity
  - Self-attention distilling for extreme long sequences
  - Generative style decoder for one-forward-operation prediction

### 6. **Transformer Architecture and Attention Mechanisms in Genome Data Analysis** (2023)
- **Authors**: Sanghyuk Roy Choi, Minhyeok Lee
- **Focus**: Application of transformers to genomic data
- **Scope**: Comprehensive review of 2019-2023 developments in genome analysis

### 7. **A Survey on Vision Transformer** (2022)
- **Authors**: Kai Han et al.
- **Coverage**: Comprehensive review of vision transformers
- **Categories**: Backbone networks, high/mid/low-level vision, video processing
- **Focus**: Self-attention mechanisms in computer vision

## Key Trends and Innovations:

1. **Efficiency Improvements**: Multiple papers focus on reducing computational complexity (ProbSparse, PDE-guided, sparse attention)

2. **Long Sequence Handling**: Several approaches for managing extremely long sequences (Continuous-Time Attention, Informer)

3. **Domain-Specific Applications**: Specialized attention mechanisms for ASR, computer vision, genomics, and time-series forecasting

4. **Theoretical Advances**: New mathematical frameworks (GPAM, PDE-based attention) addressing fundamental limitations

5. **Interdisciplinary Perspectives**: Comparative studies with human cognition and biological applications

These papers represent the cutting edge of transformer attention research, showing both theoretical advances and practical applications across diverse domains.
